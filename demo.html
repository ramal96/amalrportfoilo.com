Fragmented perspective

This cubist painting transforms the familiar shape of bottles into a kaleidoscope of fragmented planes and colors, capturing the essence of these objects from multiple angles in a single, intricate composition. The layering creates a maze-like structure, inviting viewers to lose themselves in its complexity and discover new details with each glance.The layering and abstraction emphasize the dimensionality and complexity of the bottles, creating a dynamic interplay between form and space.

About Cubism

Cubism is a revolutionary art movement that emerged in the early 20th century. In oil painting, Cubism involves a radical departure from traditional perspectives, breaking subjects down into geometric shapes and presenting multiple viewpoints within a single composition.

Radiance of Innocence

This digital painting captures the pure joy and innocence of a child’s smile, brought to life with the chiaroscuro technique. Chiaroscuro, emphasizing the interplay of light and shadow, is used here to highlight the child’s soft facial features against a dark background. The bright light on one side illuminates the gentle curls and the child’s lively expression, creating a striking contrast. This technique not only accentuates the joyful mood but also gives depth and dimensionality to the portrait, making the expression appear almost lifelike, as if capturing a fleeting, heartwarming moment in time.


About Chiaroscuro

Chiaroscuro is an artistic technique that employs strong contrasts between light and dark to create a sense of volume and three-dimensionality in two-dimensional works. The term comes from the Italian words "chiaro," meaning light, and "scuro," meaning dark. This technique is often used in painting and drawing to enhance the dramatic effect of a scene, highlight certain elements, and guide the viewer's attention.
